Introduction

Ticketmaster Entertainment, Inc. is an American ticket sales and distribution company. Ticketmastser’s current touring tickets are at a fixed rate. Our purpose of this project is to evaluate the effects of geographic locations, event types and tickets purchase time on the popularity of the event tickets. We focused on the most popular events, “Beyonce - The Formation World Tour”, “Dave Matthews Band” and “Coldplay- A Head Full Of Dreams Tour.

Methods

  1. We plot the top 3 events with the most event tickets sold according to their purchase locations.

  2. We use outside sources to investigate the relationship between popular purchase location and local average residence income.

  3. We plot tickets sold by state and compared the total tickets sold and the tickets sold per 100,000 populatoin in each state.

  4. We plot tickets sold versus difference between time of purchase and time of delivery for all events, and we repeat the same for the 3 most representative events.

Population and Tickets Sold

Further analyses show that the total tickets sold in each state are positively correlated with its population. California, New York, and Texas which are 3 of the 4 most popular states sold the most tickets, and Nevada and Massachussetts sold the most relative to their population.

Future Directions and Implications

It is demonstrated above that location and population of a state are important factors that are relevant to the total tickes sold and should be considered when constructing a pricing optimization model. Several states, such as California and New York, generally sell better than the other states, and raising ticket prices in these states could potentially increase the revenue. Moreover, tickets were sold closer to the event dates, suggesting another price optimization possibility.
